**Letter against intellectual property**<br/><br/>

First, we need to talk about knowledge.

How do products of work of one's mind come in possession of society and become humanity's heritage? Through communication. We can know about the reasonings of a 19th-century philosopher because of notes he left. Thus we can reproduce his thoughts in our heads. Humanity's knowledge is a system of individual carriers of knowledge, communicating with each other. For example, the philosophical circle studied works of their predecessors, collectively developed some positions, and made those positions available to humanity through protocols of the meetings or by implication in the following works. Thus it's absurd to claim the results of reasoning work because it's collective and can't be different.

The system of individual carriers of knowledge does not mean only people in the spirit of physical systems. There is a whole lot of techonologies (tools, transport, communication), social (academia, school, library) and economic (production, trade) relations - medium that allow to obtain and spread knowledge. Cognition depend on science and technology as social phenomena, on social relations itself. It's seperate interesting topic: how we should manage science and techonologies, organize society to spread the knowledge in most efficient way.

In our society stories about overcoming poor starting conditions and achieving eventual success are praised. In case of science it's a story about Indian mathematician Ramanujan. There shouldn't be a cult of personality, who obtained knowledge against the system, without having full access to knowledge. There shouldn't be a cult of Ramanujan. There is no such thing as genius. It wasn't genius, that allowed him to overcome a lack of mathematical education and rediscover and discover many mathematical formulas and theorems. He worked out a book for university entrants. It was sufficient for him to obtain knowledge sufficient for his following work. The absence of proofs in this book defined the style of his reasoning. He didn't develop math from anything. First, he learned reading and writing, which, obviously, created collectively, and then a compilation of works of many generations of mathematicians in form of the book for the entrants. Later he moved to Cambridge. There he worked with the leading mathematician of the time - Hardy. Thus you can't say that Ramanujan redeveloped mathematics. There shouldn't be a cult of overcoming, genius person, but the cult of people, knowing as much as possible. "Genius" shouldn't be luck, but an inevitable result of system operation. So we don't have a little group of people, who were fortunate to obtain skills and knowledge sufficient for success, but a whole society of these people.

So-called liberals think it's OK to block access to full texts of textbooks, films, music, and then they swear they speak for the comprehensive development of individuals. They offer access to a little fraction of books copyrighters generously bestowed to be open source.

No one possesses the knowledge, that historically had formation through the work of generations of scientists and writers, who worked on surplus value, produced by the exploited classes. Any knowledge that doesn't concern the personal life of living or recently living people is the heritage of humanity. Any artist in his mind, any true scientist desire to convey the products of his work to the greater audience. They are forced to paywall their works to get the emolument because they don't have the other options of payment for their work.

You must be paid for the labor of writing works important for culture and science, but the practice of getting an emolument for your work must be eliminated. It's in possession of society. You shouldn't get anything from the distribution of your work. You must get paid for your labor. If readers decide to do so, they sponsor your initiatives.

In today's world with technologies of decentralized and open distribution of information, restrictions justified with payment issues look far-fetched.

Knowledge shouldn't be a commodity.

Scan the book and share it - tear it out of the nets of commodity production.

About "Ideas can not be copyrighted. Only expressions." Ideas are conveyed through expressions, so you can't know about the idea if all expressions are closed.

I'll say it again. No artist would want to restrict access to their works. And for what? For your studio to profit from your audience, get the most of the emolument and give you a fraction of it for you to work, demand you to work on the production line to sell poorly made films, music, etc.
